Dona Paula,Goa

Okay so lady falls in love with a fiserman. Father prohibits marriage. Lady takes her life. That's Dona paula's story in two[1] sentences for you. It's a classic tragedy. One that causes a kind soul to wonder if there's any fairness in the world. In these moments one tends to become a little brooding and melancholy. If life was a movie then it wouldn't be an inappropriate time to play mozarts Violin Sonata K.378 in the background.

Experinece teaches one that in such moments of sadness it is important to have a "glass half full" perspective about you. It is undeniably true that a great injustice had been done. But life goes on.

A refreshing sip from a cool coconut followed by gentle swipe on the touchscreen brings up a wikitravel guide. The following sentence stood out:

"A myth says Dona Paula entombed in the Cabo Chapel is sipposed to be seen emerging from the moonlit waves wearing only a pearl necklace "

The monkeys on the upper floor started paddling hard. A bulb lighted up. I said to myself -- "Akshat,old firend, when a lady gives you an opportunity to see her pearls you don't sit around and muse on life. These things are best reserved for a rainy day, which are plentiful by the way. This is the time to stand up and take action. The monkeys might be on to something here. Who knows when one might get lucky?"

And so it was decided. Dona Paula deserved a through exploration. It had my full attention. I started with The jetty.
