Observation and deduction

"You see but you do not observe".

How simple it all sounds when Sherlock does it. From the shake of a hand he's able to deduce that the person has been to Afganistan. From a glance of the boots he's able to conclude the occupation of a man. From the sound of the steps he's able to tell who is at the doorstep.

His methods and tricks seem so natural that one often forgets that he was just fiction and tires to emulate him in real life. No doubt once or twice you've walked up to a friend and remarked about how his sleeve had a stain on the left corner and then went on to ask . Also no doubt your friend is generally not pleased to see you in Holmes' character as to try to look at your "observing and deducing" best while smoking an imaginary pipe.

But we should not let small stumbles get in the way of our understanding the methods of Sherlock Holmes. We all owe it to our friends. They were very patient with us while we made a fool of ourselves. We can't give up when the things get tough. It would be an insult to freindship.

Imaginary as he no doubt was Sherlock still got his logic right. His ability to think critically and his expertise in focusing on the most important details won him followeres and acclaim. The bar he has set for us is very high. We are not aiming to become like him. All we are trying to understand is how he did what he did. All we want to do is to know how tall the mountain really is?

Sherlock's method

Sherlock Holmes reasoned about his cases in two distinct phases. In the observational phase he'd go about collecting facts, looking for clues and carefully gathering information from as many sources as necessary. During this period he'd try not to form any concrete opinion about the solution to the problem he was trying to solve. Many times Watson would ask Holmes the meaning of their latest discovery but Holmes would rarely throw any light on the matter.

The other phase which he called deduction invovlved an ellimination of possibilites that couldnt' happen untill only one remained, however improbable, that explained the facts.

Of course obtaining facts by itself is not of much use unless one has way to extract useful information from them. Holmes overcame this by a meticulous study on a wide variety of subjects. He had deep knowledge of chemistry, poisons, handwriting, geography soil composition, knew obsucre languages and in short there was hardly a subject where holmes would find himself out of his depth.
