Observation and deduction

Sherlock Holmes reasoned about his cases in two distinct phases. In the observational phase he'd go about collecting facts, looking for clues and carefully gathering information from as many sources as necessary. During this period he'd try not to form any concrete opinion about the solution to the problem he was trying to solve. Many times Watson would ask Holmes the meaning of their latest discovery but Holmes would rarely throw any light on the matter.

The other phase which he called deduction invovlved an ellimination of possibilites that couldnt' happen untill only one remained, however improbable, that explained the facts.

Of course obtaining facts by itself is not of much use unless one has way to extract useful information from them. Holmes overcame this by a meticulous study on a wide variety of subjects. He had deep knowledge of chemistry, poisons, handwriting, geography soil composition, knew obsucre languages and in short there was hardly a subject where holmes would find himself out of his depth.
