Recently I came upon this article called the truth about political corectness .

"It’s just that many people no longer find those jokes funny"

I am starting to get a bit confused here. Should jokes be made potically correct because the audience does not find them funny anymore? Or should they be made politically correct because it is the right thing to do?

There is quite a bit of difference between the two. For the former there is no way that the author can speak for all kinds of audiences. There may or may not be a healthy amount of people who find politically incorrect jokes funny. The only way to find out would be by doing a through research. But since no such reseacrh was cited I ask myself "did the author just put in those words to make it sound convincing?" If that is the case then I'd also like to make a statement:-

People are tired of all these lame politically correct jokes.

For the latter case that is the politically incorrect jokes shouldn't be made because they are wrong then one needs to ask oneself wrong in what sense? Lets take the general arguments made against them:-

1. Because they tend to exclude people

Bars tend to exclude people who don't drink alcolhol. Should the bars be closed? Swimming pools are not kind to people who don't know how to swim. Should we shut down all the swimming pools? What do people afraid of heights feel about bungee jumping? Should we also take into account the sentiments of a claustarophobic person regarding an underground cave?

The jokes can be hurful. Especially if they are taken personally. It's also something that is not as easy to overcome as say a physical challange of running 30 Kms. Then it will be indifference and then excessive politeness, then the food, then the roads, then the water, then the weather and in short every small or big thing will make you unhappy. Unless you put in a sincere effort to be happy. It does not come cheap you know. It's the costliest thing in the world. You put your thouhghts to maximize your happiness and you'll forget about things that make you unhappy or those that hurt.

3. It should't be like this-- people should be supportive and caring

In an indeal world may be. But that perfection does not exist. Nither in an individual nor in a society. I suppose in a way it's admirable to try to change the attitudes of people.
