SAP Hybris

Hybris is a software for commerce,marketing,revenue and sales.

Features

  1. The Customer Service Cockpit pulls customer data together in one place, allowing customer service agents to serve their clients’ needs regardless of channel. [We can replicate this feature with our forum helpdesk idea, even hybris has a forum integration with sap jam that they encourage their users to buy]
  2. The internationalization helps brands to serve an audience around the world
  3. Manage content – and your entire catalog – from one place.
  4. Manage different businesses from a single platform, including your relationships with different suppliers, distributors and partners.
  5. Make order processing across all channels quick and easy, including complex arrangements such as bulk ordering, order replenishment and multi-channel fulfillment.
  6. Enable B2B customers to manage their own accounts and place orders. They can assign rights to employees, set up purchasing limits, and maintain individual budgets.

Lafayette Case Study observations

[Hybris did a case study with a major french fashion store. The study highlights how hybris solution was used to replace their old crufty electronic store. I note down my observations below.]

  1. Apparently its hard for a company with billion dollars in revenue to create a simple ecommerce platform (which is nothing more than a content management system really).The in house team could not even build a software that allows dynamic updation of store catalogue [I find it hard to swallow but it indicates that there is a market for dedicated ecommerce platforms. Amazon is not the be all end all of electronic stores].
  2. The language of the study indicates that the product was custom built for the store. Its as if every feature of the product was designed specifically to alleviate their pain [Thats what every company wants their users to believe] .For ex the study boasts that the brand is known for listening and responding to their customers' needs and therefore the brand went with their customer service cockpit product. However I believe the real reason they signed up for it was because one of cockpit's sales representative pushed the feature too hard, the "brand" had too much money to burn and they were just too exhausted with their day to day operations to deliberate on IT decisions. So they let the "experts" chose for them. [And it's quite plain to see how hybris has gone to great lengths to establish their expertise in the market by conducting seminars and publishing papers and doing a PR story on founders struggling to put the idea out when they were just little kinds in college. Standard stuff.]. We won't be taking this approach but there are still some lessons to be learnt on the art of product building/selling.
  3. The project took 9 months to complete. [9 months? aha!]
  4. 40 million unique visitors in 2013. [We should be able to use this number as the test data]

YAAS

[YAAS exposes sap hybris components as a service [They call it CAAS i.e. commerce as a service] that allows its customers to have more control over the product development. Every small/big thing that goes into making an ecommerce application is exposed as a service to the developers. Pricing is based on number of api calls per month. So the api calls are monitored]

  1. Microservice based architecture. Either build new applications or extend existing ones. API based approach.
  2. "you can focus on building an amazing customer experience".yaas recognizes that a unique user interface would be important for stores to get their branding right so they encourage the users to use their api driven service. To facilitate the UI building part they have created opensource templates that simplifies the process. Brilliant.
  3. Instant provisioning, autoscale and mashup are key selling points. Users can pick and use the components they want.
  4. YAAS also offers a marketplace where developers can publish their own apps that can be used by other customers with a pay per use model.

Klarna

Notes

  1. Klarna offers a developer api platform. The api allows you to do orders management, integrate a checkout page and payments page. Standard stuff.
  2. Klarna has a public shop directory and a public case study of businesses. Some of the customers on that page can become an ideal target for our forum software that can help them build communities just like the sap jam platform.

Klarna as an ecommerce platform has limited information to give. Its main focus is is on the payments and it's done quite a bit of work on building seamless checkout experiences. Our focus should be to study the customers of klarna and see the common functionality that we can replicate from them. Also explore the possibility if we can sell any of our other services to them.

Reaction

  1. It's a single page app which means that it should be ideal for small shop owners, especially our target audience of vacation rental providers. More importantly the app is built ground up to facilitate customization of the store. They believe in the brand value of the product.
  2. It's being built as a conversion first platform. What this means is that a retailer will be able to offer dynamic promotions, pricing and personalized merchandising.
  3. The reaction forums are a good example of how a community can be created around an application. [They started the discussion even before the app was out and posted topics not only related to their platform but also that helped other people by mentioning various resources that can aid in learning about the platform and the tools upon which its built.]

[Since it runs on mongo db we need to be really careful of our deployments. Too many mongo db instances can quickly eat up our available ram. Plus it seems that there will be lots of websockets involved so that means more server resources, we need to build our pricing scheme appropriately]

[A hosted platform is offered at $2500 a month. We can build our own usage based pricing scheme like yaas/shopify]

[We'll start by selling the service to people who don't need to ship their products. i.e just like forums it'll start it's life as an integration with ellobed. Later on we can figue out the shipping method integrations and offer the platform to people who have to ship physical products]
