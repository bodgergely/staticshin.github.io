Why Estonia? [Application for e-residency program]

I'm Akshat,I'm an entrepreneur living in India. I'm building a startup that allows independent home owners to promote and manage their rental properties. As an E-resident of Estonia I hope to administrate an international company from anywhere in the world and sell our products not only to tour operators and accommodation providers within Estonia but also expand to other EU countries. With its emphasis on e-governance , its support to international startups , it's vibrant tourism industry that is connected with entire EU region, Estonia makes for an ideal country for us to run our business in.

Notes on the innovation summit

The summit costs RS 2700 for a non member. It's not a lot of money but attending conferences is mostly a waste of time. The real networking happens after the conference. During the conference three things are absolute:-

  1. No one will remember what you say to them, so that when you contact a person again you'll have to introduce yourself once more.
  2. You'll be spending a lot of time inside closed rooms being bored to death. (It's worse than lectures at college)
  3. The speakers on the stage act like celebrities, "Oh I forgot to bring my card sorry (XO)","Oh could you drop me a line on my email?","What is your value proposition?"

So what are we going to do?

We're going to play it smart. We could crash but even crashing is not worth it (unless you count the free food), but we can try for the thrills <3. The speaker list,the attendee list and even the programme details are public. We can use our imagination cook up a story and get a meeting.

-- Akshat Jiwan Sharma
